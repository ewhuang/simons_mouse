We have already observed that clusters from the networks with GO have much higher GO enrichments than clusters from the networks without GO. This can be seen in the attached graph.
However, we want higher GO enrichments without sacrificing in-density/out-density
ratio.

In our last meeting, we decided that a good way to determine if the clusters with GO meet our criteria is by
1. For clusters without GO, find the median in-density.
2. Take 0.8 of this median.
3. Find all clusters from the network with GO that have in-density above this median.
4. Find out which of these clusters have GO nodes.

We define the weight for each gene-GO edge as follows.
current_go_weight = max(log(lambda * largest_go_size / current_go_size), 1.0)
For lambda = [0.5, 0.6, 0.7, 0.8, 0.9, 1.0], I performed the experiment above.
The results are lists of numbers, where each number corresponds to a cluster with GO, and the number means the number of GO terms in that cluster.

The median in-density of the clusters without GO was 0.011886. It is the same for every network without GO.
